# thermio-deploy-IOT
Real-Time Indoor Thermal Comfort Monitoring and Analysis Using IoT Sensors
Abstract
Indoor thermal comfort significantly affects human health, productivity, and well-being. This study focuses on analyzing indoor environmental conditions using IoT-based sensors. The system considers parameters such as temperature, humidity, and air quality to evaluate thermal comfort levels.

Introduction
Indoor environments play a crucial role in maintaining human comfort. Variations in temperature, humidity, and air quality can directly impact health and performance. This research aims to study and analyze these environmental factors using IoT-based monitoring techniques.

Objectives
To analyze indoor environmental conditions
To evaluate thermal comfort levels based on collected data
To study the impact of temperature, humidity, and air quality
Methodology
Environmental data such as temperature, humidity, and air quality is considered for analysis. The data is either collected or simulated and then evaluated to determine indoor thermal comfort conditions. The analysis focuses on identifying comfortable and non-comfortable environmental states.

Parameters Considered
Temperature
Humidity
Air Quality Index (AQI)
Analysis
The collected data is analyzed to observe patterns in indoor conditions. Comfortable ranges are identified based on standard environmental conditions, and deviations are studied to understand their impact.

Results
The analysis shows that indoor environments remain comfortable when temperature and humidity are within specific ranges. Variations in these parameters affect overall comfort levels.

Conclusion
This research highlights the importance of monitoring indoor environmental conditions. The study demonstrates that analyzing temperature, humidity, and air quality can help in understanding and improving indoor thermal comfort.

Future Scope
Integration with real-time IoT systems
Advanced data analysis using machine learning
Development of smart indoor monitoring solutions
